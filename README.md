# 💭 Nau — Personal Cognitive Analysis AI

> "Existing AI tools analyze people. Nau helps people understand themselves."

---

## Overview

Most AI tools apply the same frameworks to everyone.
Nau works differently.

Nau is a personal cognitive analysis AI that connects 
individual patterns — how you process sensory input, form 
thoughts, perceive space, and respond physically — to 
established frameworks in cognitive science, psychology, 
and physiology.

The goal is not to diagnose or advise.
The goal is to explain: not *what* you're experiencing, 
but *why* you work the way you do.

---

## The Problem

Existing tools share a common limitation.

- **Mental health apps**: focused on symptom management, 
  applying standardized CBT frameworks to all users
- **Personality tests**: static results with no explanation 
  of underlying mechanisms
- **AI journaling tools**: emotion pattern tracking with 
  limited theoretical grounding
- **Self-tracking apps**: data collection without explaining 
  the "why" behind the patterns

The deeper issue is this: existing AI tools are optimized 
for the average user. They apply the same frameworks 
to everyone.

But some people process the world differently. They absorb 
sensory input more deeply, perceive space through bodily 
sensation, hear sounds as scenes, and think in layers 
rather than lines. For those people, standardized 
analysis doesn't fit.

None of these tools answer the real question:

*"Why do I react this way — and what does that actually mean?"*

Nau was built to answer that question, grounded in real 
research rather than generic frameworks.

## What You Can Do

**Understand your reactions**
Why you get overwhelmed in certain environments, why specific 
sounds are harder to filter, why your energy drops on humid 
days. Nau connects lived experience to real mechanisms.

**Translate abstract inner patterns into language**
Patterns that are hard to describe — how you perceive space, 
how you process people, how your thoughts flow — get mapped 
to cognitive science frameworks and explained in usable terms.

**Analyze physical responses**
Why your shin hurts after long walks, why your heart races 
when waking up, why brain fog occurs. Physiological 
mechanisms explained based on established research.
*(Axis 10: Physical & Physiological Responses)*

**Decision support**
Describe a situation with multiple options. Nau maps out 
possible outcomes based on your cognitive patterns — without 
making the decision for you. The final choice stays yours.

**Schedule optimization**
Ask how to sequence tasks. Nau considers your sensory 
processing tendencies and energy patterns to suggest an 
order that fits how you actually work.

**Cumulative memory**
Every conversation deepens Nau's understanding of you. 
Patterns sharpen over time. Observations from months ago 
connect to what you say today.

## How It Works

Nau is structured around a personal profile built across 
10 cognitive axes, each grounded in published research.

| Axis | Domain |
|------|--------|
| 1 | Sensory / Perceptual Processing |
| 2 | Person Perception |
| 3 | Memory Style |
| 4 | Thought Flow & Metacognition |
| 5 | Emotion–Creativity Connection |
| 6 | Social Adaptation Patterns |
| 7 | Spatial Cognition |
| 8 | Bodily / Motor Sensation Processing |
| 9 | Interoception |
| 10 | Physical & Physiological Responses |

**Theoretical foundations include:**
Auditory Scene Analysis (Bregman, 1990) ·
Sensory Processing Sensitivity (Aron, 1996) ·
Metacognition (Flavell, 1979) ·
Peripersonal Space (Rizzolatti) ·
Polyvagal Theory (Porges, 1994) ·
Episodic Memory (Tulving) ·
Mirror Neuron System (Rizzolatti & Gallese) ·
Interoception (Damasio) · and more.

**Response structure:**
1. Plain-language explanation
2. Theoretical depth with research connections
3. Synthesis
4. Follow-up question
